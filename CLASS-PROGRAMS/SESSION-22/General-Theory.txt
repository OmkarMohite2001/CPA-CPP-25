Oeprators can be classified based on their ARY. 
(ARY -> Number of operands required by the operator to function correctly)

C++ has three kinds of operator based on their ary. 

1) UNARY 
    1) Dereference opeator  (*ptr)
    2) Logical NOT  (!cond)
    3) BITWISE NOT  (~n)
    4) Arrow operator (ptr->member) 
    5) Call operator (functionName(parameters))
    6) Subscript operator (a[i])
    7) Pre-increment    (++a)
    8) Pre-decrement    (--a)
    9) Post increment   (a++)
    10) Post decrement   (a--)
2) BINARY 
    1) Addition                     (a + b)
    2) Subtraction                  (a - b)
    3) Multiplication               (a * b)
    4) Division                     (a / b)
    5) Modulo                       (a % b)
    6) Less than                    (a < b)
    7) Less than or equal to        (a <= b)
    8) Greater than                 (a > b)
    9) Greater than or equal to     (a >= b)
    10) Equal to                     (a == b)
    11) Not equal to                (a != b)
    12) Logical AND                 (a && b)
    13) Logical OR                  (a || b)
    14) Bitwise AND                 (a & b)
    15) Bitwise OR                  (a | b)
    16) Bitwise XOR                 (a ^ b)
    17) Shift arithmetic left       (a << b)
    18) Shift arithmetic right      (a >> b)

3) TERNARY 
    (cond) ? (expr1) : (expr2)

4) Special opearators 
    1) cout operator    (std::cout << n)
    2) cin operator     (std::cin >> n)
    3) new operator     (new T(parameters))
    4) delete operator  (delete ptr) 

5) Operators which cannot be overloaded 
    1) Scope resolution:    (std::cout)
    2) dot operator         (myDate.getDay())
    3) Ternary              (cond) ? (expr1) : (expr2)
    4) Sizeof               sizeof(int)
    5) Typecast             (T)expr
    6) ampersed &           &num; 
    7) comma operator       int num1 = 10, num2 = 20;
